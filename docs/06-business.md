# 06 · Business — Roadmap, Monetization, Expansion, Long-Term Vision

## Feature roadmap

### v1.0 — "The Number" (launch, Norway first)
The wedge: the world's best answer to "what can I safely spend?"

- Open Banking connections (Norwegian banks via Neonomics/Tink, EU next)
- Safe-to-Spend engine + derivation sheets ("Why?" everywhere)
- Horizon 30/60/90-day projection with confidence bands + dip Signals
- Commitments: bill & subscription detection, payday cycle
- Goals stack (up to 3 free), buffer as first-class goal
- Purchase Simulator (basic verdict + goal impact)
- Signals (the 3-gate notification system)
- Steady Score with full decomposition
- Onboarding "reveal", discreet mode, export & delete

*Launch bar: a new user reaches their live Safe number in < 90 seconds,
and the first week of Signals contains zero noise.*

### v1.5 — "The Twin"
- Full Digital Twin: saved scenarios, ghost lines, stress test
- Purchase Simulator: best-date engine, alternatives, reservations
- Subscription Intelligence complete (price rises, trials, duplicates)
- Pilot proactive cards + natural-language search
- Horizon to 365 days · receipt scanning

### v2.0 — "The Household"
- Households: shared pots, roles, partner-safe privacy model
- Shared goals & household Horizon; "who-contributes-what" fairness view
- Teen accounts (view + allowance rules) — the family OS begins
- Sverige/Danmark launch; multi-currency polish

### v2.5+ — "The Platform"
- Debt module: payoff strategies simulated on real cash flow
- Investments & pension read-in (net-worth Horizon; never stock-picking)
- Mortgage twin: rate-change stress tests, refinance timing
- Public read-only API + widgets/watch/CarPlay-class surfaces
- UK/EU broad rollout; US via Plaid when the wedge is proven

## Monetization — aligned or nothing

Principle: **we charge for the engine, never for the user's own data,
and never take money that biases advice.** No ads, no kickbacks, no
selling "insights." (Rocket-style savings cuts and referral steering are
permanently off the table — misaligned incentives are the category's
disease, and our immunity is the brand.)

- **Free is genuinely good** — the full Safe number forever. It's the
  trust builder and the growth engine; a crippled free tier would
  poison word-of-mouth in a category built on trust.
- The paywall moment is *ambition*, not function: you pay when you want
  the year-long view, the saved futures, and the coach — upgrade prompts
  appear only at natural desire moments ("see the whole year?"), never
  as interstitials.
- Annual pricing anchors; students half price; a hardship policy (keep
  Plus free if unemployment is detected — the moment they need us most
  is the worst moment to paywall; it's also unbeatable brand).

### Later revenue (still aligned)
- **Business tier** (sole proprietors: irregular-income smoothing, tax
  set-aside as a commitment type — Norway's freelancer market is
  underserved).
- **API/platform licensing** for the Kernel (banks license the engine
  the way they license Tink for data).
- Never: interchange plays, credit brokering, data products.

## Growth strategy

1. **The screenshot is the funnel.** "Safe to spend: 4,280 kr" is
   self-explanatory virality — every share of the (amount-hidden,
   brag-safe) milestone cards markets the core promise.
2. **The stress-test hook:** "How long would your money last?" is a
   free, shareable, 60-second onboarding path that converts anxiety into
   a signup without a single ad-tech trick.
3. **Nordic beachhead:** small, rich, Open-Banking-mature, design-
   literate markets where word-of-mouth density is achievable; win
   Norway visibly, let the case study open EU.
4. **Referrals that fit the brand:** give a month of Plus, get a month —
   framed as "give a friend a calmer month."
5. Retention is the strategy: the daily 8-second habit + monthly Annual-
   Letter-quality moments target 6-month retention > 60%, and D30 > 40%
   — a subscription business is won on year two, not install day.

## Future expansion (beyond 3 years)

- **Life-event packs:** first home, first child, divorce, retirement —
  curated Twin templates with local rules (Norwegian parental leave,
  BSU, skattemelding awareness).
- **Income OS for the irregular economy:** freelancers, creators, gig
  workers — smoothing volatile income into a steady personal "salary" is
  a product in itself.
- **Household fairness engine:** proportional-to-income splitting,
  transparent and negotiable — quietly one of the most requested and
  least solved problems in couples' finance.
- **Voice/ambient surfaces:** "Hey, can I afford this?" answered on
  watch/earbuds at point of sale.
- **Kernel-as-a-service:** the deterministic projection engine licensed
  into banks and employers (financial wellness benefits) — MoneyOS
  inside, brand intact.

## Long-term vision

**Year 1:** own one number. Become the definitive answer to "what can I
safely spend?" in one country.

**Year 3:** own the future tense. When any European asks "can I afford
X?" — a purchase, a move, a career change — the reflex is to ask their
Twin. Households run on shared Horizons.

**Year 10:** the trust layer for personal finance. Banks hold the money;
MoneyOS holds the *understanding*. The Kernel is the standard engine for
"what happens next" — in the app, in banks that license it, in benefits
programs — while the covenant (deterministic math, explainable numbers,
zero data monetization) remains unbroken and unbreakable, because it is
the product.

The end state was in the name from day one: not an app you check, but
the **operating system your financial life quietly runs on** — and the
metric that proves it will never appear on a dashboard: millions of
people who simply stopped being afraid of their money.
